The Six Tastes of Ayurveda

Every meal should ideally contain all six tastes to fully nourish the body and prevent cravings. Each taste has specific effects on the three Doshas.

🍯

Sweet (Madhura)

Nourishes tissues, calms Vata & Pitta. Rice, wheat, dairy, dates, root vegetables.

🍋

Sour (Amla)

Stimulates digestion, awakens appetite. Lemon, fermented foods, yogurt, tamarind.

🧂

Salty (Lavana)

Hydrates, improves taste, aids digestion. Rock salt, seaweed, mineral-rich foods.

🌶️

Pungent (Katu)

Stimulates metabolism, clears congestion. Ginger, black pepper, chilli, garlic.

🌿

Bitter (Tikta)

Detoxifying, anti-inflammatory. Neem, turmeric, bitter melon, leafy greens.

🫐

Astringent (Kashaya)

Toning, drying, anti-inflammatory. Lentils, pomegranate, apples, turmeric.

🌬 Vata Diet — Warm, Moist & Grounding

Vata is cold, dry, light, and mobile. Your diet should be the opposite — warm, oily, heavy, and nourishing. Regularity is key: eat at the same times every day.

✓ Favour These Foods

  • Warm cooked meals — soups, stews, porridge
  • Ghee and sesame oil generously
  • Root vegetables — sweet potato, carrot, beet
  • Ripe sweet fruits — bananas, mangoes, dates
  • Whole grains — rice, wheat, oats
  • Warm spices — ginger, cumin, cardamom
  • Warm milk with turmeric and honey
  • Soaked almonds and walnuts

✗ Avoid These Foods

  • Raw vegetables and salads
  • Cold drinks and ice cream
  • Dry crackers, popcorn, raw nuts
  • Beans and lentils (unless well-spiced)
  • Bitter and astringent foods in excess
  • Caffeine and carbonated drinks
  • Skipping meals or fasting
  • Eating while anxious or on the go
💡 Vata Tip: Never skip breakfast. Eat your meals warm and at regular times. Avoid eating while distracted or stressed. An Abhyanga (self-massage) with warm sesame oil before your morning meal helps ground Vata energy.

🍲 Vata-Balancing Recipes

Warm, oily, and grounding — these recipes nourish and calm scattered Vata energy.

🥣
Warm Spiced Oat Porridge
⏱ 15 min🍽 1 serving🌅 Breakfast

A grounding, warming breakfast that steadies Vata's erratic energy. The ghee and cardamom deeply nourish the tissues.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • 1½ cups warm water or whole milk
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • ¼ tsp cardamom powder
  • ¼ tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp soaked raisins
  • 1 tsp raw honey (added after cooking)
  • Pinch of rock salt
Method
  1. Bring water or milk to a gentle simmer in a small saucepan.
  2. Add oats, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt. Stir continuously on low heat for 8–10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in ghee and soaked raisins.
  4. Once slightly cooled (not hot), drizzle honey on top. Serve immediately.

Mung Dal & Sweet Potato Soup
⏱ 35 min🍽 2 servings🌙 Lunch / Dinner

A classic Ayurvedic one-pot meal. Yellow mung dal is the easiest legume to digest and the sweet potato provides grounding nourishment.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup yellow split mung dal (rinsed)
  • 1 medium sweet potato, cubed
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp ginger powder
  • Rock salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander to garnish
Method
  1. Rinse dal and pressure cook with sweet potato and water for 3 whistles (or simmer 25 min until soft).
  2. In a small pan heat ghee; add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add turmeric, coriander, and ginger; stir 30 seconds.
  4. Pour the tempering into the dal. Add salt and stir well.
  5. Simmer together 5 minutes. Garnish with coriander and serve hot.

Golden Turmeric Milk (Haldi Doodh)
⏱ 10 min🍽 1 serving🌙 Evening Drink

The most beloved Ayurvedic tonic. Deeply nourishing, anti-inflammatory, and perfect for calming Vata before sleep.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup whole milk (or oat milk)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ¼ tsp cardamom powder
  • ¼ tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of black pepper (activates curcumin)
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • 1 tsp raw honey (add after heating)
Method
  1. Warm milk gently in a saucepan on low heat — do not boil.
  2. Whisk in turmeric, cardamom, cinnamon, and black pepper.
  3. Add ghee and stir until melted and fragrant.
  4. Pour into a cup and once slightly cooled, stir in honey.
  5. Drink 30–60 minutes before bed for best results.

Ghee Rice with Cumin & Cashews
⏱ 25 min🍽 2 servings☀️ Lunch

Simple, sattvic, and deeply satisfying. Basmati rice cooked in ghee with warming spices is the ideal Vata meal.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup basmati rice (soaked 20 min)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4–5 cashews
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • 2 cups water
  • Rock salt to taste
Method
  1. Heat ghee in a heavy pot. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and cashews; fry 1 minute until golden.
  2. Add drained rice and turmeric; stir gently to coat every grain with ghee.
  3. Add water and salt; bring to boil, then reduce to lowest heat.
  4. Cover tightly and cook 15 minutes. Remove from heat; rest 5 minutes before fluffing.

🔥 Pitta Diet — Cooling, Sweet & Hydrating

Pitta is hot, sharp, oily, and intense. Your diet should cool and calm this fire — sweet, bitter, and astringent tastes are your allies. Avoid skipping meals, which aggravates Pitta.

✓ Favour These Foods

  • Coconut and coconut milk
  • Cucumber, zucchini, leafy greens
  • Sweet fruits — melons, grapes, pomegranate
  • Basmati rice and wheat
  • Cooling spices — coriander, fennel, mint, cardamom
  • Dairy — milk, ghee, butter (room temp)
  • Cilantro and rose water
  • Lime juice in moderation

✗ Avoid These Foods

  • Spicy foods — chilli, garlic, raw onion
  • Alcohol and caffeinated drinks
  • Red meat and fried foods
  • Fermented foods — vinegar, sour cream
  • Sour fruits — green grapes, citrus in excess
  • Salt in excess
  • Eating when angry or stressed
  • Skipping meals (causes irritability)
💡 Pitta Tip: Your digestive fire is strongest at noon — eat your largest meal at lunchtime. Avoid working through lunch. Take a short walk in nature after eating to calm Pitta energy.

Cucumber Coconut Raita
⏱ 10 min🍽 2 servings☀️ Lunch Side

Deeply cooling and easy to prepare. The combination of cucumber and coconut directly counters Pitta's heat and acidity.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup plain full-fat yogurt (room temperature)
  • ½ cucumber, grated and excess water squeezed out
  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut or fresh coconut
  • ½ tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Pinch of rock salt
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
Method
  1. Whisk yogurt in a bowl until smooth.
  2. Fold in grated cucumber, coconut, and mint.
  3. Season with cumin, coriander, and salt.
  4. Chill for 10 minutes before serving alongside rice or flatbread.

Coriander & Coconut Milk Rice
⏱ 30 min🍽 2 servings☀️ Lunch

A sattvic, mildly fragrant rice cooked in cooling coconut milk — perfect as a Pitta-pacifying main meal base.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup basmati rice (soaked 20 min)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp ghee or coconut oil
  • ½ tsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves
  • Rock salt to taste
Method
  1. Heat ghee and temper fennel seeds for 30 seconds.
  2. Add drained rice and coriander powder; stir 1 minute.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and water; add salt and bring to boil.
  4. Reduce heat, cover, and cook 15 minutes on very low flame.
  5. Rest 5 minutes off heat, fluff and garnish with fresh coriander.

Sweet Fennel & Rose Lassi
⏱ 5 min🍽 1 serving🌅 Afternoon Drink

A cooling digestive drink that calms heat, aids digestion, and refreshes mid-afternoon — the ideal Pitta tonic.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • ½ cup cold water
  • ½ tsp fennel powder
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • 1 tsp raw sugar or mishri (rock candy)
  • 2–3 fresh mint leaves
  • Pinch of cardamom
Method
  1. Blend yogurt, water, fennel, rose water, and sugar until frothy.
  2. Taste and adjust sweetness — it should be pleasantly sweet, not tart.
  3. Pour over a little ice (optional for Pitta in summer).
  4. Garnish with mint and a pinch of cardamom. Sip slowly.

Mint-Coriander Mung Dal

Light yet nourishing. Mung dal tempered with cooling coriander and mint keeps Pitta calm and digestion smooth.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup split yellow mung dal

  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp turmeric

Method

  4. Add rock salt, lime juice, and fresh herbs. Serve warm with rice.

🌍 Kapha Diet — Light, Spicy & Stimulating

Kapha is heavy, slow, cold, and moist. Your diet should be the opposite — light, dry, warm, and stimulating. Kapha types often do well skipping breakfast or eating it very lightly.

✓ Favour These Foods

  • Light grains — barley, millet, corn, rye
  • All vegetables especially bitter and leafy greens
  • Legumes — lentils, chickpeas, mung beans
  • Heating spices — ginger, black pepper, turmeric, mustard seeds
  • Honey (raw, unheated) as sweetener
  • Light fruits — apples, pears, cranberries
  • Warm water with ginger and lemon
  • Fermented foods in moderation

✗ Avoid These Foods

  • Heavy dairy — cheese, yogurt, ice cream
  • Wheat and white bread in excess
  • Sweets and sugary foods
  • Fried and oily foods
  • Cold drinks and refrigerated food
  • Overeating or eating without hunger
  • Red meat and heavy proteins
  • Excessive salt
💡 Kapha Tip: Eat only when genuinely hungry. Intermittent fasting suits Kapha well. Begin each morning with warm ginger lemon water to kindle digestive fire. Vigorous exercise before meals significantly boosts Kapha metabolism.

🌶️ Kapha-Balancing Recipes

Light, spicy, and stimulating — these recipes awaken Kapha's sluggish digestion and lift its heavy energy.

🫚
Ginger Lemon Morning Tonic
⏱ 5 min🍽 1 serving🌅 Morning Ritual

The single most powerful Kapha-reducing daily habit. Ignites digestive fire, clears morning ama, and energises the lymphatic system.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup warm water (not boiling)
  • ½ inch fresh ginger root, grated or sliced
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 tsp raw honey (add after water cools slightly)
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • Pinch of turmeric (optional)
Method
  1. Steep grated ginger in warm water for 3–4 minutes.
  2. Strain if desired, then add lemon juice and black pepper.
  3. Once water is just warm (not hot), stir in honey.
  4. Drink on an empty stomach, 30 minutes before breakfast.
🫘
Spiced Red Lentil Soup
⏱ 30 min🍽 2 servings☀️ Lunch

Light, high-protein, and loaded with warming spices. Red lentils are perfect for Kapha — easy to digest yet deeply satisfying.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup red lentils (rinsed)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp coconut oil or dry-roasted (no ghee for Kapha)
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • Rock salt and lemon juice to taste
Method
  1. Boil lentils with turmeric and water until soft (20 min).
  2. Heat oil; splutter mustard seeds, then add cumin, ginger, garlic, and pepper.
  3. Fry 2 minutes, add spinach, wilt 1 minute.
  4. Combine tempering with lentils; simmer 5 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and a generous squeeze of lemon. Serve with barley roti.

Warm Barley & Roasted Veg Bowl
⏱ 40 min🍽 2 servings☀️ Lunch

Barley is the ideal Kapha grain — light, dry, and scraping. Paired with roasted bitter vegetables, this bowl is deeply balancing.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup pearl barley (soaked 30 min)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1 cup bitter gourd (karela), sliced thin
  • 1 tsp olive oil or dry roast
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • Rock salt and lemon juice
Method
  1. Cook barley in 1.5 cups salted water until tender (25 min). Drain excess water.
  2. Toss vegetables with oil, pepper, chilli flakes, and salt. Roast at 200°C for 20 minutes.
  3. Combine barley and roasted vegetables in a bowl.
  4. Drizzle with lemon juice and coriander powder. Serve warm — not hot.

Trikatu Herbal Tea
⏱ 8 min🍽 1 serving🌅 Morning / After Meals

Trikatu (three pungents) is Ayurveda's most celebrated Kapha remedy. This tea burns ama, stokes Agni, and clears respiratory congestion.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup water
  • ¼ tsp dry ginger powder (shunthi)
  • ¼ tsp black pepper powder
  • ¼ tsp long pepper (pippali) — or substitute extra black pepper
  • 1 tsp raw honey (add after cooling)
  • A few tulsi leaves (optional)
Method
  1. Bring water to a boil; add ginger, black pepper, pippali, and tulsi.
  2. Simmer on low for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain into a cup; let cool until just warm.
  4. Stir in raw honey. Drink after meals or first thing in the morning.

Seasonal Eating (Ritucharya)

Ayurveda's seasonal regimen teaches us to adapt our diet with each season to stay in harmony with nature's rhythms.

🌸

Spring (Vasanta)

Kapha melts and moves — favour light, dry, warm foods. Bitter vegetables, barley, honey, ginger tea. Avoid heavy dairy and sweets. A good time for a light detox.

☀️

Summer (Grishma)

Pitta peaks — favour cooling, hydrating foods. Coconut water, sweet fruits, cucumber, mint. Avoid spicy, fried, or overly salty foods. Eat smaller, cooler meals.

🌧️

Monsoon (Varsha)

Digestive fire is weakest — favour easy-to-digest, warm, freshly cooked foods. Moong dal, rice, ginger soup. Avoid raw foods, leafy greens, and heavy proteins.

❄️

Winter (Hemanta)

Digestive fire is strongest — the best season to eat heartier, nourishing foods. Ghee, warm milk, sesame, root vegetables, dates. Enjoy warm soups and stews freely.

The 10 Golden Rules of Ayurvedic Eating

These timeless principles from classical Ayurvedic texts form the foundation of healthy digestion. Follow them consistently and your body will reward you with energy, clarity, and lasting wellness.

Rule 01 🍲

Eat Freshly Cooked Food

Freshly prepared food carries the highest Prana (life force). Avoid reheated, canned, or processed food whenever possible — food loses its vital energy within hours of cooking.

🌿 Prana · Sattvic Eating
Rule 02 🕐

Eat at Regular Times

The body's digestive enzymes peak at predictable intervals. Irregular eating destroys the body's rhythm and creates Ama (undigested toxins) — the root cause of most disease in Ayurveda.

⏱ Dinacharya · Daily Routine
Rule 03 🤲

Eat Only When Hungry

Never eat on top of an undigested previous meal. Wait until genuine hunger signals arise — not just habitual or emotional cravings — before eating again.

🔥 Agni · Digestive Fire
Rule 04 🧘

Eat in a Calm Environment

Never eat while anxious, angry, or distracted. Your emotional state directly impacts how food is digested and assimilated — a stressed mind creates a stressed gut.

🌸 Manas · Mind-Body Connection
Rule 05 ☀️

Largest Meal at Noon

Digestive fire (Agni) mirrors the sun — it peaks at midday and weakens by evening. Your largest, most complex meal should always be lunch, never dinner.

☀️ Agni · Solar Rhythm
Rule 06 🌙

Light Dinner Before 7:30 PM

As the sun sets, so does Agni. A heavy evening meal is one of the leading causes of Ama accumulation, weight gain, and poor sleep quality in modern lifestyles.

🌙 Ratricharya · Evening Regimen
Rule 07 💧

Sip Warm Water with Meals

Warm or room-temperature water aids digestion gently. Cold drinks extinguish the digestive fire mid-meal — one of the most common and easily correctable causes of poor digestion.

💧 Jala · Hydration Wisdom
Rule 08 ⚖️

Eat the Right Quantity

Fill ⅓ of your stomach with food, ⅓ with water, and leave ⅓ empty for digestive fire to work. Overeating is described in Ayurveda as the primary direct cause of disease.

⚖️ Matra · Portion Wisdom
Rule 09 🥛

Combine Foods Wisely

Avoid incompatible combinations (Viruddha Ahara): milk with fish or meat, fruit with dairy, honey with ghee in equal quantities — these create toxins even when individual foods are pure.

🧬 Viruddha Ahara · Food Combining
Rule 10 🚶

Walk Gently After Meals

A gentle 10–15 minute walk after eating significantly improves digestion, regulates blood sugar, and aids assimilation. Never lie down immediately after eating.

Quinoa Paneer Vegetable Pulao

Ingredients
