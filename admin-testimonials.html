Google Form (Recommended)

Formal, documented consent — best for compliance & records

Send this link to patients after a positive consultation. The form collects their testimonial, star rating, and explicit written consent in one step. Responses feed directly into your Google Sheets for easy import here.

▶ How to set up your Google Form
  1. Go to forms.google.com → click Blank form
  2. Title: "Share Your Experience with Chakradev Ayurveda"
  3. Add fields: Full Name (Short answer) · Your Experience (Paragraph) · Star Rating (Multiple choice: ★ to ★★★★★) · May we display this on our website? (Checkbox: Yes, I consent)
  4. Click Send → copy the link → paste it above
  5. Link the form to a Google Sheet to track all responses in one place

Legal & Ethical Guidelines for Testimonial Collection

✅ Consent is Mandatory

Always obtain explicit written consent before publishing any testimonial. The Google Form checkbox ("I consent to Chakradev Ayurveda displaying this on their website") constitutes valid written consent. A verbal WhatsApp "yes" alone is not sufficient — always follow up with the form.

🔒 Privacy & Anonymisation

Publish only first name + last initial (e.g. Priya M.) unless the patient explicitly consents to their full name. Never publish phone numbers, email addresses, or any identifying health details beyond what the patient has voluntarily shared in their testimonial.

📋 AYUSH & Consumer Law

Under the Drugs & Magic Remedies Act, 1954 and Consumer Protection Act, 2019, health testimonials must not claim to cure, treat, or prevent any disease. Testimonials should reflect personal experience only. Always include the standard disclaimer on the public page.

🚫 What Not to Do

Never fabricate, paraphrase beyond meaning, or embellish a testimonial. Do not use pressure tactics to solicit testimonials ("Leave a review or we won't continue your treatment"). Do not offer discounts or gifts in exchange for positive reviews — this is considered deceptive advertising under Indian law.

🗂 Record Keeping

Maintain a consent record for every published testimonial — the date consent was given, the method (form, email, WhatsApp screenshot), and the patient's name. Use the Export CSV function monthly as your compliance record. Keep records for a minimum of 3 years.

📝 Required Website Disclaimer

The following disclaimer must appear on every page displaying testimonials: "Individual results may vary. These testimonials reflect personal experiences and do not constitute a guarantee of treatment outcomes. Ayurvedic treatments are complementary to, and not a replacement for, conventional medical care."

Legal references: Drugs & Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisements) Act, 1954 · Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (Section 2(28) — Misleading Advertisements) · Information Technology Act, 2000 (Data Privacy) · AYUSH Ministry Guidelines on Advertising, 2023. For legal advice specific to your practice, consult a qualified healthcare advertising lawyer.
